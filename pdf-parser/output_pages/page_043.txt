Yes OB-Obstetric Emergencies No If crown showing and amniotic sac is intact, pinch sac and twist membrane to rupture For otherwise uncomplicated, head-first delivery: • Assist normal delivery. Do not push or pull the newborn • Protect perineum with gentle hand pressure • Support and control head as it emerges • Check for cord around neck. If present, manually reduce or clamp/cut cord • Wipe mouth and nose with clean towel • As shoulders emerge, gently guide head/neck downward to deliver anterior shoulder. Then gently lift head/neck to deliver posterior shoulder • Remainder of infant’s body should deliver easily – maintain firm grip on baby • Clamp/cut umbilical cord. Delay of at least 60 seconds is recommended if neonate does not require immediate intervention/resuscitation If delivery does not progress normally, e.g. head OB-Obstetric Yes delivers but shoulders do not Emergencies Postpartum Care • Placenta should deliver in 20-30 minutes. Do not pull umbilical cord.
