OB-Obstetric Emergencies Facility notification General Adult Assessment telemetry immediately upon recognition of OB E Administer oxygen to all patients Emergency A Vascular Access • Discourage pushing by mother • Elevate mother’s pelvis (knee to chest, Trendelenberg, or pillows) Prolapsed umbilical cord • Place gloved hand into the vagina and gently push presenting fetal part off the cord. Maintain this position until relieved by hospital staff • Wrap exposed cord in warm, saline-soaked dressing • Discourage pushing by mother Presentation of single limb • Elevate mother’s pelvis (knee to chest, Trendelenberg, or pillows) • Do not attempt delivery • Support infant and allow delivery to proceed while preparing for transport • It may be necessary to gently sweep the legs from the vagina if they do not deliver spontaneously • If buttocks and trunk deliver, support the body and avoid hyperextension of Breech delivery the head • If head does not deliver within 30 seconds of the trunk, place 2 gloved fingers into the vagina to locate the infant’s mouth. Push vaginal/uterine wall away from infant’s mouth to maintain airway. Maintain this position until relieved by hospital staff • Never pull on the body – support the baby’s body while mother pushes • Suspect if head delivers normally but shoulders do not deliver within 1 minute, especially if head retracts tightly to the perineum when the mother stops pushing (“turtle sign”) Shoulder Dystocia • DO NOT pull on head • Once recognized quickly perform the following: 1. Hyperflex the mother’s legs towards the abdomen and abduct the hips (McRoberts Maneuver) 2. If not successful after 30 seconds, maintain leg hyperflexion and apply form downward pressure to the suprapubic region (just above the symphysis pubis). DO NOT apply pressure to the uterine fundus, as this can cause uterine rupture or placental abruption. 3. If still unsuccessful, consider rolling mother to the “all-fours” position. Third trimester bleeding • Suspect placental abruption or placenta previa (6-8 months) • Do not perform digital examination • Place patient in left lateral position Postpartum hemorrhage • Significant ongoing bleeding after delivery with >1000 ml of blood loss • Vigorously massage uterine fundus until firm Shock Consider TRANEXAMIC ACID P 1 g IV/IO, given over 10 minutes ONLY if administration is <3 hours after delivery Continue General Adult Assessment OB-Obstetric Emergencies (revised and approved by MAB 09/03/2025) 39
