Pearls • Recommended exam: Mental Status, HEENT, Lungs, Neuro • History should include any clotting disorders such as Hemophilia or Von Willebrand disease, as these can contribute to bleeding. • Non-accidental trauma as well as foreign body should be considered in pediatric patients with epistaxis. • It is very difficult to quantify the amount of blood loss with epistaxis • Bleeding may be also occurring posteriorly. Evaluate for posterior blood loss by examining the posterior pharynx. • Detailed medication history should be obtained to assess for any NSAIDS, Antiplatelet agents or Anticoagulant medications that may contribute to bleeding.
